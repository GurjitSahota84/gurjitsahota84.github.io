Case Study: Online Buyflow

To improve the eCommerce experience, I redesigned the existing checkout process to make it more intuitive, responsive, and transparent. The goal was to reduce cart abandonment, improve clarity around pricing and discounts, and streamline the flow across devices. We developed this mobile-friendly web application for various in house brands(Bell, Virgin, Distributel, Acanac).

Role:

Lead UX/UI Designer, I collaborated on business requirements, created low- and high-fidelity wireframes, gathered approvals, incorporated feedback, and contributed to HTML/CSS development and design integration.

Project Blueprint

Customer Actions The steps the user takes during the online purchase journey:

  • Enters e-commerce site or app >> Browses product listings
  • Selects a product >> Views comparison (price, features, discounts)
  • Adds product to cart >>Reviews cart (edits quantities, applies discounts)
  • Proceeds to checkout >> Enters payment/shipping details
  • Confirms order >>Receives confirmation

Frontstage (User Interface / Touchpoints) : Visible elements that the user directly interacts with:

  • Responsive product
  • Product detail pages with dynamic comparison modules
  • Discount badge overlays and tooltips
  • Sticky, collapsible cart accessible site-wide
  • Clear CTAs: “Add to Cart,” “Compare,” “Checkout”
  • Progress indicator in checkout flow
  • Order summary with editable line items
  • Mobile-first checkout UX
  • Confirmation screen + email confirmation

The Challenge

Cluttered and inconsistent across devices, Lacking clear discount representation, Confusing price comparisons (e.g., sale vs. original), responsible for a high drop-off rate on mobile

The Approach & Solution

  • Competitive Analysis & Benchmarking: Reviewed UX patterns from top-performing eCommerce brands
  • Identified effective UI conventions for cart and pricing clarity.
  • Wireframing & Prototyping: Tool: Figma Created low to high-fidelity prototypes for: Cart summary view with price breakdown, Step-by-step checkout (Shipping → Payment → Review) , Mobile-first responsive layouts
  • Design Implementation: Front-End: HTML5, CSS3, Flexbox, Bootstrap, Used atomic design principles for scalable UI components
  • Validation: Ran A/B tests comparing the old and new checkout flows, Tested with real users on mobile and desktop

The Results

The redesigned purchase flow not only enhanced UX clarity and reduced friction, but also contributed to a significant uplift in mobile conversions—a critical success factor for the client’s growth targets.
